General introduction

To be able to estimate locations based on light intensities recorded in high arctic/antartctic latitudes it is essential to use geolocators that recrods the entire light spectrum, allowing to detect even small changes in light intensity during periods when the sun is constantly above the horizon.

Additionally, it is highly important to have a period of recordings from known location (> 2 weeks) for calibration.

The general workflow has four steps:

  1. Calibration
  2. Spatial mask
  3. Maximum likelihood estimates
  4. Summary/Plotting

Data preparation and import

The R Package Polar Geolocation provides an example calibration dateset from a Great Knot Lisovski et al. 2016 as well as data recoded in their arctic breeding grounds.

Available data:

  1. GRNT_calib
  2. GRNT_breed

The calibration data has been recorded at deployment site, Roebuck Bay, Australia (122.27ºE, -17.96ºS).

Loading the datasets:

data(GRNT_calib)
data(GRNT_breeding)

1. Calibration

The function getTemplateCalib allows to calculate the maximum light values and the error distribution for a range of zenith angles.

Within getTemplateCalib, the following parameters can be defined:

  • tagdata - a dataframe with columns Date and Light that are the sequence of sample times (as POSIXct) and light levels recorded by the tag (e.g. GRKN_calib).
  • twl - a dataframe with columns Twilight and Rise (e.g. GRKN_twlCalib).
  • lon - calibration longitude.
  • lat – calibration latitude.
  • bin - the with of zenith ranges to estimate zenith specific maximum light value and error structure.
  • adjust - adjust twilights for tags that report the maximum light interval observed in the preceeding sampling interval.
  • plot – logical, if TRUE a final plot will be produced.
